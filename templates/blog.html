In the world of telecom forensics, a Call Data Record is not just a billing artifact — it is a timestamped diary of a person's movements, relationships, and daily routines. CDRInv.Online was built to help authorized investigation officers decode that diary quickly, accurately, and securely. This post walks through the platform's two core analysis modes: Single CDR Analysis and Dual CDR Analysis.

Analysis Mode 01

Single CDR Analysis

Upload one person's CDR and the platform immediately begins building a comprehensive picture of their life as reflected in the telecom record. From daily movement trails to dormant relationships, Single CDR Analysis surfaces what would otherwise take analysts days to uncover manually.

Movement & Location Intelligence

By mapping the BTS (Base Transceiver Station) towers a handset connected to over time, the platform reconstructs the subject's physical movements across any date range. Investigators can pinpoint exactly where the subject was on a specific date, identify their probable home or overnight location, and determine their likely workplace or regular daytime zone — all derived directly from tower visit patterns.

Communication Profile

Who a person calls, how often, and at what time of day reveals as much as where they go. The platform produces a rich communication profile — from closest associates ranked by interaction frequency to late-night call patterns and helpline usage flagged separately from person-to-person voice events.

  • Total analyzable records in the CDR
  • Total unique contacts reached
  • Closest & most frequent associates
  • Top night-time call contacts
  • Internet & helpline usage events flagged separately

Device Intelligence

If the SIM card was used across multiple handsets during the CDR period, the platform detects and flags each IMEI switch. Investigators receive a precise count of handset changes and a complete list of every device the SIM was inserted into — a critical signal for identifying SIM-swapping, borrowed phones, or attempts to evade tracking.

Why it matters: A suspect who changes phones frequently while keeping the same SIM leaves a clear forensic trail. CDRInv surfaces every IMEI transition so investigators know exactly which handset was active during any given event window.

Travel & Behavioural Trend

Beyond point-in-time location, the platform analyses the subject's overall travel trend across the full CDR timeline — revealing whether they are sedentary or highly mobile, whether their routine changed during a specific period, and how geographically dispersed their tower visits are. This trend data provides crucial context for any investigation that hinges on establishing presence or absence at a location.

Analysis Mode 02

Dual CDR Analysis

When two parties are of investigative interest, Dual CDR Analysis goes beyond each individual record to examine the relationship between them. Upload both CDRs and the platform cross-references every dimension of their data to answer the question investigators most need answered: how connected are these two people, and what does that connection look like?

Baseline Summary for Both Subjects

Subject A

Subject B

Total analyzable records from the second CDR, enabling a balanced apples-to-apples comparison of the two data sets.

Direct Contact Verification

The platform performs an exact cross-match to determine whether the two subjects ever called or messaged each other directly. This binary yes/no answer is foundational — it distinguishes between two people who know each other and two people who merely share mutual contacts.

Direct contact found? Every direct call or SMS between the subjects is timestamped and surfaced, giving investigators a precise interaction log rather than a mere association flag.

Physical Meeting Detection

Even when two subjects never call each other, they may meet in person. By correlating BTS tower data from both CDRs at the same timestamps, the platform estimates the probability of a physical meeting — identifying when, where, and under what circumstances it likely occurred. This co-location analysis is one of the most operationally powerful features in the platform.

  • Meeting probability score between the two subjects
  • Specific date, time, and BTS location of probable meetings
  • Geographic context of each meeting point

Intermediary / Middle-Man Detection

Sophisticated actors rarely communicate directly. The platform scans both CDRs for shared contacts — people who appear in the call logs of both subjects — identifying potential intermediaries or cut-outs used to relay information while keeping the principals insulated from each other. These common contacts are ranked and flagged for further investigation.

Common contacts surfaced: Every person who appears in both CDRs is identified, enabling investigators to map the full social network connecting the two subjects — even when they never spoke directly.

Relationship Strength Score

Combining direct contact frequency, co-location events, shared intermediaries, and interaction timing, the platform produces a single relationship strength indicator between the two subjects. This score gives investigators an evidence-backed, quantified basis for assessing how closely linked the two parties are — critical when building a case or prioritising follow-up targets.
